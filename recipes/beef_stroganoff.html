Beef Stroganoff Recipe


Description

Beef Stroganoff is a classic and belvoed dish that has its origin in Russian. This iconic recipe is believed to have been created in the mid-19th century and is named after the influential Stroganov family, who were prominent Russian aristocrats and patrons of the arts. The dish was originally made with thin strips of beef sauteed in a rich and creamy sauce, typically featuring ingredients like sour cream, mushrooms, and onions. Over the years, the recipe has evolved and adopted to various culinary traditions, making it a cherised comfort food worldwide. Beef Stroganoff's enduring popularity can be attributed to its luxurious and savory flavors, making it a timeless favorite that has withstood the test of time.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound beef sirloin or tenderloin, thinly sliced into strips
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish
  • Cooked egg noodles or rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet or pan, melt the butter over medium-high heat. Add the thinly sliced beef strips and cook until they are browned on all sides. Remove the beef from the skillet and set it aside.
  2. In the same skillet, add the coped onion and garlic. Saute for a few minutes until the onions are translucent and fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms to the skillet and cook them until they are tender and any liquid they release has evaporated.
  4. Sprinkle the flour over the mushroom and onion mixture and stir well to combine. This will help thicken the sauce.
  5. Pour in the beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, and optional paprika. Stir to combine all the ingredients. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for a few minutes to thicken the sauce.
  6. Return the cook beef strips to the skillet and simmer for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the beef is heated through.
  7. Reduce the heat to low, then stir in the sour cream. Make sure not to boil the sour cream, as it can curdle. Simmer gently until the sauce is heated but not boiling.
  8. Season the Beef Stroganoff with salt and black pepper to taste. Adjust the seasonings as needed.
  9. Serve your Beef Stroganoff over cooked egg noodles or rice, garnished wtih chopped fresh parsley for a burst of color and freshness.

Enjoy your homemade Beef Stroganoff, a classic and creamy comfort food!
