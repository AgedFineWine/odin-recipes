Chicken Pot Pie Recipe


Description

Chicken Pot Pie, a beloved comfort food, has a rich history that spans centuries. Its origins can be traced back to medieval Europe, where savory meat pies were a common culinary tradition. These early pies often feature a mixture of meats and vegetables enclosed in a simple pastry crust. As European settlers brought their recipes to the Americas, chicken pot pie evolved to include chicken as the primary protein, a reflection of the bird's availability and popularity. The dish gained widespread popularity in the United States during the 19th and 20th centuries, becoming a staple of American comfort cuisine. Today, chicken pot pie continues to be a cherished dish known for tis heartwarming combination of flaky pastry and creamy, savory filling, making it a timeless classic enjoyed in households across the world.

Ingredients

For the Pie Filling:

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, diced or shredded
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn, and green beans), frozen or fresh
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme (optional)
  • 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup milk

For the Pie Crust:

  • 2 store-bought pie crusts or homemade pie crusts

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425F (220C)
  2. In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
  3. Stir in the flour, salt, pepper, and optional dried thyme. Cook for a coupple of minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture becomes golden and bubbly.
  4. Slowly pour in the chicken broth and milk, stirring constantly. Continue to cook and stir until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil. This will form a creamy sauce for your pot pie.
  5. Add the cooked chicken and mixed vegetables to the sauce. Stir to combine and heat through. Remove the skillet from the heat.
  6. Roll out one of the pie crusts and place it in the bottom of a 9-inch (23 cm) pie dish. Press the crush into the dish.
  7. Pour the chicken and vegetable mixture into the pie crust.
  8. Roll out the second pie crust and place it over the filing. Trim any excess crust and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  9. Cut a few small slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  10. Bake the chicken pot pie in the preheated oven for about 30-35 mintues, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
  11. Remove the pie from the oven and let it cool for a few mintues before serving.
  12. Slice and serve your homemade chicken pot pie for a warm, comforting meal.

Enjoy this classic and hearty Chicken Pot Pie!
