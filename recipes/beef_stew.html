Beef Stew Recipe


Description

Beef stew has a history rooted in humble origins, dating back centuries. It emerged as a practical and economical way to make the most of touch cut meats, where slow cooking was needed to render them tender and flavorful. Early rendittions of beef stew can be found in medieval European cuisine, where meats, vegetables, and grains were cooked together in a pot over an open flame. As European settlers brough their culinary traditons to the Americas, beef stew evolved, incorporating local ingredients like potatoes and creating regional variations. During times of economic hardship, beef stew became a staple in many households, as it could stretch a small amount of meat to feed a larger family. Today, it remians a beloved comfort food and a testament to the art of slow cooking, offering a warm and satisfying meal with rich, hearty flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups chopped celery
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large zip-top bag, add the flour and season with salt and black pepper. Add the beef cubes to the bag and shake to coat the meat evenly with the flour.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the floured beef cubes and brown them on all sides. Work in batches if needed to avoid overcrowding the pot. Once browned, remove the beef and set it aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onions and garlic. Saute for a few mintues until they are all soft and fragrant.
  4. Return the browned beef to the pot and pour in the beef broth and water. Add the bay leaves and dried thyme. Sir to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and simmer for about 1.5 hours to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender. Stir occasionally.
  6. Add the carrots, potatoes, and cerely to the pot. Continue to simmer for an additional 30-40 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  7. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and black pepper as needed. Remove the bay leaves.
  8. Serve your delicious beef stew hot, garnished with chopped fresh parsley if desired.

Enjoy your homemade beed stew, a hearty and comforting meal, especially on a chilly day!
